Real Live Boyfriends is the fourth and final book in E. Lockhart's Ruby Oliver series, picking up with Ruby in her senior year as she navigates a complicated romantic life (yes, boyfriends plural), crippling panic attacks, and the looming anxiety of college applications. Lockhart writes Ruby with a voice that's instantly recognizable: neurotic, self-aware, funny, and prone to making lists of everything from "Boys Who Have Rejected Me" to "Things That Are Wrong With My Life." The series, which began with The Boyfriend List in 2005, built a devoted readership because Ruby feels real. She overthinks, she screws up, she keeps going.

Real Live Boyfriends has been challenged 8 times across Florida, Pennsylvania, and Texas, flagged for LGBTQ+ themes and sexual content. The LGBTQ+ objection relates to supporting characters, not the main romance, reflecting the pattern where any mention of queer characters triggers a challenge regardless of how minor the role. Lockhart's series is consistently praised by educators for its honest portrayal of teen anxiety and emotional intelligence. The idea that a book about a girl learning to manage panic attacks and trust her own judgment is too dangerous for teenagers says more about the challengers than about Ruby Oliver.
