This version of *The Odyssey* condenses one of the foundational epics of Western literature into graphic form. That means monsters, shipwrecks, gods, detours, and a long-delayed return home, all carried by a format that can pull in readers who might never start with a dense prose translation.

## Why This Book Was Banned

The challenge label here is almost certainly a catch-all. Mythology is full of sex, violence, vanity, revenge, and divine bad behavior, which is part of why it has endured for thousands of years. What matters is context, and in this case the context is plainly literary and educational.

## Why You Should Read This

A good adaptation can function like an invitation. It lowers the drawbridge to an old text without pretending the old text doesn't matter. If a reader reaches Homer through comics first, that's still a win.
