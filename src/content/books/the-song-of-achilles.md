The Song of Achilles retells Homer's Iliad through the eyes of Patroclus, an awkward, exiled prince who befriends Achilles, the golden boy destined to become the greatest warrior of his generation. Their friendship deepens into love, and when they are called to fight in the Trojan War, Patroclus follows Achilles into a conflict that will define them both. Miller's prose is luminous and devastating, turning a 3,000-year-old war story into an intimate love story.

Winner of the Orange Prize for Fiction and a massive BookTok favorite, the novel has been challenged for its sexual content (including explicit same-sex scenes), LGBTQ+ themes, and graphic depictions of war violence. It is one of the most frequently banned literary fiction titles in American libraries.
