The Love Hypothesis follows Olive Smith, a third-year biology PhD candidate who, to convince her best friend she's dating someone, impulsively kisses the first man she sees: Adam Carlsen, a notoriously grumpy young professor. When Adam unexpectedly agrees to keep up the ruse, their fake relationship becomes entangled with real feelings, academic politics, and the systemic challenges women face in STEM.

A #1 New York Times bestseller and a BookTok phenomenon, the novel has been challenged for sexual content and profanity. Its removal from some library shelves has been particularly ironic given its themes of women fighting to be taken seriously in their professional environments.
