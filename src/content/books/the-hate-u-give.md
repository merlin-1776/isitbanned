The Hate U Give centers on Starr Carter, who lives in a poor, mostly Black neighborhood but attends a wealthy, mostly white prep school. When she witnesses a police officer shoot her unarmed friend Khalil during a traffic stop, Starr is forced to navigate the chasm between her two worlds as the shooting becomes national news. Thomas' debut novel, inspired by the Black Lives Matter movement and the shooting of Oscar Grant, is a fierce, compassionate examination of systemic racism, police violence, and what it means to find your voice when every institution is telling you to stay quiet.

The Hate U Give has been one of the most challenged books in America since its 2017 publication, with over 180 total challenges. A school district in Katy, Texas pulled it from library shelves; Central York School District in Pennsylvania banned it along with other books by or about people of color before student protests forced the district to reverse course. Challengers cite its profanity, depictions of drug dealing, and what they call "anti-police" messaging. The book has been removed from libraries in Texas, Florida, South Carolina, Virginia, Pennsylvania, and Oklahoma. Thomas has noted that the real-world events her novel describes, police killings of unarmed Black people, are far more graphic and disturbing than anything in her pages.
