Ellen Hopkins's The You I've Never Known alternates between prose and verse to tell the story of Ariel, a teenager whose controlling father has moved her from town to town her entire life. As Ariel begins to explore her attraction to a girl named Monica, she also starts questioning the stories her father has told her about her absent mother. The novel weaves together queer identity and parental abuse into a single narrative about a girl learning that the truths she's been told aren't true at all. Hopkins balances two timelines and two formats with the structural ambition that characterizes her best work.

The You I've Never Known was challenged 9 times in the 2024-2025 PEN America data, with bans in Florida, Tennessee, and Texas school districts. The challenges cite LGBTQ+ themes, sexual content, and violence. The book adds a queer coming-of-age story to Hopkins's extensive catalog of banned titles, combining the LGBTQ+ content that drives one category of book bans with the frank treatment of abuse that drives another. Hopkins has said that Ariel's story came from letters she received from young readers in similar situations: controlled by a parent, questioning their sexuality, and unable to access the truth about their own lives.
