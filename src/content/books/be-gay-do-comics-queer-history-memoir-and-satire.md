This anthology from The Nib brings together comics about queer history, memoir, satire, culture, and politics. Instead of offering a single argument, it gives readers a chorus of voices, drawing styles, and lived experiences. That variety is part of the point.

## Why This Book Was Banned

The objections tend to collapse everything into the vague label of "LGBTQ content," which tells you more about the censors than the book. What bothers challengers is not explicitness so much as visibility. A collection like this makes queer people legible in public, on the page, and in history.

## Why You Should Read This

It gives readers something rare: breadth. Humor sits next to grief, politics next to intimacy, history next to everyday life. If somebody wants to ban a book for showing that queer people have always been here and have always made art, that's a strong reason to read it.
