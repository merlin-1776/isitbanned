Instructions for Dancing follows Evie Thomas, who stops believing in love after her parents' divorce. When she discovers a mysterious book at the library and suddenly gains the ability to see the beginning, middle, and end of any couple's love story just by touching a romance novel, she sees that every love ends in heartbreak. But when she meets X, a fellow ballroom dance student, she must decide whether a beautiful love story is worth it, even knowing how it ends.

A New York Times bestseller by the author of Everything, Everything and The Sun Is Also a Star, the novel has been challenged for sexual content and profanity. It appears on PEN America's Index of School Book Bans.
