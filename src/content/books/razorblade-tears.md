Razorblade Tears is a revenge thriller following Ike Randolph and Buddy Lee Jenkins, two ex-cons who had nothing in common except their sons. Their sons, one Black and one white, were married to each other. When both young men are murdered, the two fathers, neither of whom had fully accepted his son's sexuality, set out together to find the killers. In doing so, they're forced to confront their own prejudices, their pasts, and a conspiracy that reaches higher than they expected.

The novel has been challenged for graphic violence, LGBTQ+ content, profanity, and sexual content. A New York Times bestseller and one of Barack Obama's favorite books of 2021, it explores homophobia, racism, and the possibility of redemption through a propulsive noir plot.
