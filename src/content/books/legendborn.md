Legendborn follows Bree Matthews, a Black teenager who enrolls in an early college program at UNC Chapel Hill after her mother's death. When she witnesses a magical attack, she's drawn into the Legendborn, a secret society of students who claim to be descendants of King Arthur's knights. As Bree digs deeper, she discovers that her own family's history is intertwined with a different, older magic, one rooted in her Black Southern heritage.

The novel has been challenged for its depictions of violence, occult and magical themes, and sexual content. Its exploration of how white institutions have co-opted and erased Black history, told through the lens of Arthurian legend, has made it both a Coretta Scott King Honor Book and a target for removal from school libraries.
