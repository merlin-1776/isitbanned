House of Earth and Blood launches Sarah J. Maas's Crescent City series, set in a modern-feeling world populated by angels, shapeshifters, fae, and demons. Bryce Quinlan, a half-fae party girl, teams up with Hunt Athalar, a fallen angel enslaved by the ruling Archangels, to investigate the murder of Bryce's best friend. At 800 pages, the book blends urban fantasy, romance, murder mystery, and political intrigue into a dense, addictive package. Maas published this through Bloomsbury's adult imprint, positioning it clearly outside YA, but her existing fanbase followed her across the aisle.

House of Earth and Blood was challenged 9 times in the 2024-2025 PEN America data, with bans in Florida school districts including Broward, Escambia, Hillsborough, Palm Beach, Pinellas, and Santa Rosa County. As an explicitly adult fantasy novel, its presence on school library ban lists highlights the blurriness of the Maas problem: her adult books get shelved near her YA books, and her YA readers age into her adult work without a clear boundary. The challenges cite sexual content and violence, both of which are more intense than in her YA work. Whether these books belong in high school libraries is a legitimate question; whether they should be banned is a different one entirely.
