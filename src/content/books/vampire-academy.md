Richelle Mead's Vampire Academy built a massive YA following with its mix of supernatural action, boarding school drama, and slow-burn romance. Rose Hathaway is a dhampir (half-human, half-vampire) training to be a guardian for her best friend Lissa Dragomir, a royal Moroi (mortal vampire). When they're dragged back to St. Vladimir's Academy after running away, they face political intrigue, a mysterious threat to Lissa, and Rose's growing attraction to her older instructor, Dimitri Belikov. Mead's vampires come with their own elaborate social hierarchy, complete with a dangerous underclass of undead Strigoi who serve as the series' primary antagonists.

Vampire Academy was challenged 9 times in the 2024-2025 PEN America data, with bans across multiple school districts. The challenges cite sexual content, violence, and religious/occult themes. The book's student-teacher romantic tension (Rose and Dimitri) and its supernatural worldbuilding both draw objections, though the series handles the age-gap romance with more awareness than many of its contemporaries. Mead's six-book series and its spinoff trilogy have sold millions of copies worldwide, and the franchise has been adapted into both a film and a Peacock TV series. Despite its mainstream success, it remains a reliable fixture on ban lists.
