House of Salt and Sorrows retells the fairy tale of the Twelve Dancing Princesses as gothic horror. Annaleigh Thaumas is one of twelve sisters living in a crumbling manor on a cliff above a churning sea. Four of her sisters have already died. When the surviving sisters begin sneaking out to attend mysterious balls, Annaleigh suspects something supernatural is luring them to their deaths. As she investigates, the line between reality and nightmare begins to dissolve.

The novel has been challenged for violence, sexual content, and occult themes. A New York Times bestseller, it has been removed from school libraries in Texas and Florida as part of broader bans targeting YA fantasy.
