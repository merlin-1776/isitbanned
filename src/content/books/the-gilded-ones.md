The Gilded Ones follows sixteen-year-old Deka, who lives in a patriarchal society where girls must undergo a blood ceremony to prove their purity. When Deka's blood runs gold instead of red, she is marked as impure and sentenced to death. But a mysterious woman offers her a choice: die or join an army of girls like her, the alaki, near-immortal warriors fighting to protect the empire. As Deka trains, she discovers that everything she was taught about her world, and about herself, is a lie.

A New York Times bestseller and an instant critical favorite, The Gilded Ones has been banned for its graphic violence, sexual content, and themes of the occult. The novel's exploration of misogyny, religious hypocrisy, and female power has made it a target in multiple school districts.
