Nineteen Minutes tells the story of a school shooting in a small New Hampshire town, weaving together the perspectives of the shooter, his victims, their families, and the community left to pick up the pieces. Picoult examines the roots of bullying and social exclusion with unflinching honesty, asking uncomfortable questions about complicity and whether violence can ever be understood without being excused.

With 98 bans in the 2023-24 school year alone, Nineteen Minutes was the single most banned book in America that year. Challengers cite its graphic depictions of violence and a sexual scene between teenagers. The bans have swept through school districts in Florida, Texas, Iowa, Utah, and beyond, with entire states implementing broad removal policies that swept Picoult's novel off shelves alongside dozens of other titles.
