Tiffany D. Jackson's Grown is a thinly veiled response to the R. Kelly case, telling the story of Enchanted Jones, a talented seventeen-year-old singer recruited by Korey Fields, a superstar R&B artist twice her age. What begins as a dream opportunity curdles into a nightmare of manipulation, isolation, and abuse. When Enchanted wakes up in Korey's bed covered in his blood with no memory of what happened, she has to piece together the truth about a night she can't remember and a relationship she couldn't escape. Jackson writes about grooming with forensic precision, showing how predators isolate their victims incrementally.

Grown was challenged 11 times in the 2024-2025 PEN America data, with bans in Florida and Tennessee school districts including Knox County, Monroe County, Oak Ridge, and Rutherford County. The challenges cite sexual content, violence, and language. The book was written specifically to help teenagers recognize the signs of grooming, a form of abuse that depends on the victim not having language for what's happening to them. Jackson has pointed out that removing Grown from libraries takes away a tool that could help young people identify and resist predatory behavior, precisely the outcome that the adults claiming to protect children should want to prevent.
