Sick Kids in Love follows Isabel, a sixteen-year-old with rheumatoid arthritis, and Sasha, who has Gaucher disease. Both are tired of being defined by their conditions, and when they meet in a hospital waiting room, they make a pact: no love stories. They're just friends. That pact doesn't last. The novel is notable for centering disabled characters' joy and romance without making their illness the tragedy.

The novel has been challenged for sexual content, drug references (medication-related), and profanity. Its honest portrayal of chronic illness and teenage intimacy has made it a target for removal from school libraries.
