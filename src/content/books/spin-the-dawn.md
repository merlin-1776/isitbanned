Spin the Dawn follows Maia Tamarin, a talented seamstress in a land inspired by imperial China. When her ailing father is summoned to court to compete for the role of imperial tailor, Maia disguises herself as a boy and goes in his place. She must sew three impossible gowns, from the laughter of the sun, the tears of the moon, and the blood of stars, while hiding her identity and her growing magical abilities.

Described as Mulan meets Project Runway, the novel has been challenged for violence and sexual content. It appears on PEN America's list of banned school books and has been removed from shelves in Texas and Florida school districts.
