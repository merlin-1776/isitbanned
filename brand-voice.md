# IsItBanned Brand Voice
**Created:** 2026-04-18  
**Purpose:** SEO agent voice foundation for IsItBanned

## Voice & Tone
- Defiant, clear, and specific
- Pro-reader, anti-censorship, without sounding hysterical
- More watchdog than activist slogan machine
- Willing to say what is happening plainly: books are being challenged, removed, suppressed, or targeted
- Wry when it fits, but never cute about censorship
- Direct sentences, short paragraphs, hard facts
- Human and morally awake, not corporate and not academic mush

## Positioning
IsItBanned is a searchable public-facing database that helps readers quickly find out whether a book has been banned or challenged in the United States, where it happened, and why.

The site exists because censorship spreads faster when ordinary readers have to dig through fragmented reports, PDFs, advocacy sites, and local news coverage to get basic answers.

The promise is simple:
- search the book
- see what happened
- understand why it was targeted
- decide for yourself what to read

## Core Topics
- banned books in the United States
- challenged books and school-library removals
- censorship trends by state, district, and title
- why specific books get targeted
- reader freedom and access to literature
- frequently challenged authors and genres
- books about race, gender, sexuality, identity, religion, and history that are commonly challenged
- how book bans differ from ordinary collection decisions

## Customer Language
People using this site are likely to ask things like:
- Is this book banned?
- Why was this book banned?
- Where was this book challenged?
- Is this banned in schools or banned everywhere?
- What books are being banned in my state?
- What are the most challenged books right now?
- Why do people want this book removed?

Language patterns to mirror:
- banned books
- challenged books
- removed from schools
- banned in [state]
- why was [book] banned
- most challenged books
- books they don’t want you to read

## Things We Never Say
- We do not sound neutral about censorship in the abstract while hiding the stakes
- We do not use fluffy activist clichés or empty slogans
- We do not write like a legal memo unless the topic requires precision
- We do not say “in today’s world” or other dead filler phrases
- We do not exaggerate facts we can’t support
- We do not pretend every challenge is identical when the details differ
- We do not write generic AI sludge like “delve,” “landscape,” “robust,” or “game-changer”

## What Competitors or Adjacent Sources Get Wrong
- They bury the answer under advocacy copy, PDF reports, or hard-to-search lists
- They track censorship but do not make single-book lookup fast and obvious
- They often tell you that censorship is bad, but not clearly what happened to the specific title you searched
- Many pages are written for institutions, librarians, or reporters, not ordinary readers

## Unfair Advantage
- Searchable single-book lookup instead of abstract reporting alone
- Structured banned/challenged book data that is easier to extract, compare, and cite
- Clear reader-first framing: the best response to a ban is still reading the book
- Can blend fast answer blocks with broader trend context, making it useful for both human readers and AI citation systems

## Writing Rules for SEO Content
- Answer the query in the first paragraph
- Distinguish clearly between banned, challenged, restricted, and removed when possible
- Use names, locations, years, and reasons wherever available
- Prefer direct headings like `Why was [Book] banned?` and `Where has [Book] been challenged?`
- Include cited stats from ALA, PEN America, or other primary sources when making broader claims
- Keep moral clarity, but ground it in specifics

## Only-You-Could-Write-This Check
A strong IsItBanned page should feel like it came from people who:
- understand the censorship landscape
- care about the freedom to read
- know readers need a straight answer fast
- respect nuance without blurring the truth

If the draft could just as easily be for any generic education or library blog, it failed.
